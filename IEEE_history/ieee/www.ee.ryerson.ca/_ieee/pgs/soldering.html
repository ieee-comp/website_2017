Soldering

Author: Ram | Source: IEEE RU | 01.08.2005

    Introduction


    The main type of soldering iron that you will be using is an electric powered soldering iron.

    Procedure

    Step 1:

  • Secure the work that is going to be soldered

    • Step 2:

  • Make sure all parts being used including the soldering iron is clean to allow for a good joint.
  • Dirt that could be present, such as oxidation, on the iron tip or on the ends of terminal devices such as resistors.
  • Oxidation may be removed via a filing tool.
  • Increasing the heat of the soldering iron will not rid joint of dirt.

    • Step 3:

  • Make sure the soldering iron is coated with solder by applying a small amount of solder so that it may be used.

    • Step 4:

  • Heat all the parts with the soldering iron before soldering.
  • So if you were to solder one end of a resistor to a wire, heat both of the ends and then connect the two heated ends.
  • Do not just heat one end, only the resistor end, and try to create the joint.

    • Step 5:

  • Now heat the connection for a small amount of time where solder can be placed on the joint.
  • This is done by heating the joint and placing a small amount of solder on the joint.
  • Remember to remove the soldering iron so that the joint can cool.
