QUESTION#2: CHRISTMAS TREE LIGHTS
Difficulty: not-rated

2. As the holidays approach, many of us are getting around to trimming the tree. The lights are usually the first thing to go up, after we've untangled the strands and, theoretically, tested them to make sure that they're still working. Whether you've worked with them or not, you'll have to be up on your seasonal lighting trivia to answer this question:

Which statement about Christmas tree lights is not true?

a) The first electric ones were made by an Edison employee in 1882.

b) The first mini-lights were made with bulbs from a telephone.

c) A modern mini-light strand won't light up if a bulb is blown.

d) LED lights cost about 90% less to operate than mini-lights.
