THE IEEE ELECTRICAL ENGINEERING ONLINE QUIZ

QUESTION#6: BLACK BOXED CIRCUITS
Difficulty: not-rated

ANSWER IS:

The two circuits are electrically identical.

  • At DC, the capacitor blocks current and only the resistor on the inductor branch conducts current, so all you see is 1-ohm.
  • At very high frequencies, the capacitor looks like a short and the inductor blocks current, so only the resistor on the capacitor side is measurable.
  • At intermediate frequencies, the current is split but the total is always the same as a 1-ohm resistor. See the detailed analysis, below.

So how can you determine which is which? You have to be tricky. Here are a few sneaky answers:

  • Expose the boxes to a magnetic field and look for pickup by the inductor.
  • Weigh the two boxes. Unless the box maker was tricky, the one with four components would weigh more.
  • Expose the boxes to an electrostatic field and look for pickup by the capacitor.
  • Apply a brief, high voltage pulse and see if the characteristics change. A pulse should not affect the resistor but if the capacitor is an electrolytic, would short the capacitor, after which the two boxes would be easy to distinguish.
  • Open the boxes with a hacksaw!
  • Bribe the guy who made the boxes.
