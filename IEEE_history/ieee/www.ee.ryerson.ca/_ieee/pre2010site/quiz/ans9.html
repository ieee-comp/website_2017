QUESTION#9: THE GREAT WHITE WIZARD
Difficulty: not-rated

ANSWER IS:

No matter how you choose the first number, the sum of the digits in the difference is a multiple of nine. So when you delete a digit from the difference and tell the wizard the sum of the remaining digits, he thinks, “What's the next highest multiple of 9?” The difference between the number and the number you gave him is the deleted digit. You can confuse the wizard by choosing your first tow number so that their difference contains a 0 or a 9, and then deleting that digit. The sum of the remaining digits is still a multiple of 9, so the wizard cant tell whether you deleted a 0 or a 9.
